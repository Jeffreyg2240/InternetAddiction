About Us

This website was developed by two students who used the internet for an average of 7 hours a day.
Although 7 hours may seem excessive, in actuality a majority of that time goes towards our school work and job. Many people associate the number of hours that we spend on our online to the likelihood of addiction, but in reality, this is false. There are many factors to addiction and the time spent online isn’t a reliable factor. Internet addiction could be a side effect of other mental illnesses or bullying. Self-awareness is the best solution to this problem and denial will only make it worst. Although we may spend 7 hours a day online, we don’t feel a compulsive urge to use the internet as the online addicted may and we wanted to debunk that myth and spread awareness of internet addiction and the organizations surrounding it.

Internet Fact: The average amount of time a US High School student spends in class is 6.64 hours, not including the extracurriculars. Plus from personal experience of my school, on average a sophomore and juniors have at least 1 class using the computer lab per day, while seniors may around 2 classes, and freshmen, depending on their elective may not have any.
